Our Core Services

Comprehensive solutions for seamless project execution across Africa

🤝

Project Matchmaking

Connecting local governments, parastatals, and institutions with foreign companies who have the expertise or capital to implement large-scale projects.

🌍

Market Entry Support

Helping foreign companies understand African markets, laws, and logistics with representation, translations, and comprehensive onboarding.

🔍

Due Diligence & Risk Analysis

Verifying companies and government departments involved in projects, ensuring compliance with local laws and international standards.

👥

Stakeholder Engagement

Managing relationships with ministries, councils, NGOs, and communities involved in project implementation.

📊

Project Coordination

Monitoring timelines, budgets, and logistics on both ends after successful matchmaking and initial agreements.

💡

Consultancy Services

Offering development advisory services to donors, banks, and institutions needing trusted local partners.

How We Work

Our proven 6-step process ensures successful project execution

01

Opportunity Sourcing

Identify and profile projects from local governments, NGOs, and private companies

02

Project Profiling

Conduct background checks and define project scope, timeline, and legal framework

03

Partner Matching

Connect opportunities with vetted foreign companies, investors, and development partners

04

Connection Facilitation

Manage introductions, NDAs, meetings, translations, and comprehensive onboarding

05

Compliance & Risk Checks

Ensure regulatory clearance, environmental compliance, and contract soundness

06

Project Coordination

Monitor progress, manage logistics, and ensure smooth communication throughout

🧠 Key Stakeholders We Engage

🔹 On the Local Side

  • Government ministries (Infrastructure, ICT, Water, Agriculture, etc.)
  • Municipal councils & district commissioners
  • NGOs & parastatals
  • Local private companies needing foreign partners

🔹 On the Foreign Side

  • International companies seeking entry into Africa
  • Donor agencies (e.g. USAID, GIZ, JICA, DFID)
  • Development banks (World Bank, AfDB)
  • PPP investors, tech, engineering, construction firms
